Taiwan Lighthouse



Sandiaochiao Lighthouse is located at Sandiaochiao Mountain top in the northeast of Taiwan. During the Spainish time, the Spanish Army chased the aboriginees and established a Spanish Town called San Diego. The Chinese farmers subsequently renamed it Sandiaochiao and this name was used until now.

East Taiwan sea condition off this coastal area is very rough with steep rocky ocean floor. More Japanese vessels sank in 1929 and 1931 caught the attention of the Japanese government decided to build the lighthouse in 1931 and was completed in May 1935.



Yuwengdao Lighthouse

The lighthouse was originally built in 1778 as Xiyu Station during the Guangxu Emperor reign, a stone tower which currently has a stele as a record in the Ching Dynasty. The current lighthouse was built in 1875 and painted in 1915. It was opened to the public in 1992.
