Taiwan Lighthouse

The first lighthouse in Taiwan, the Xiyu Lighthouse, was first built in 1778 and was funded by the private sector. During World War II, all the lighthouses were destroyed; in 1947, all the lighthouses and light poles were fully restored to normal light-emitting operations.

At present, there are 36 lighthouses in Taiwan. Furthermore, there are 12 open sightseeing spots. There are 20 domestic lighthouses with a history of more than 100 years, and there are 8 in 60 to 100 years.

Yuwon Island, Donchu Island, Dongyin Island and Wuqiu Lighthouse are national monuments; Pengjiayu, Baishayu and Kaohsiung Lighthouses are county and city landmarks; Gaomei, Goose Nose, Mudouyu and Ludao Lighthouse are historical buildings.
